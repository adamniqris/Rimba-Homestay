Since 2016

Built by the Rahman family, still run by them.

The house was Pak Rahman's grandfather's — teak floors, a zinc roof that sings when it rains, and a garden that's mostly durian and pucuk ubi. When the family stopped farming full-time, they opened three of its rooms to travellers instead of selling the land.

Everything here is still run the way it always was: breakfast is whatever's in season, the WiFi password is written on a coconut shell by the door, and Pak Rahman will happily point you to the waterfall trail if you ask.
